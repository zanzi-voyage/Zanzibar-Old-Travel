PRISON ISLAND

Prison Island, also known as Changuu Island, is one of the most fascinating tourist attractions in Zanzibar. Despite its name, the island was never used as a prison for criminals. Instead, it has a rich history that includes quarantine purposes and later became a conservation area and tourist destination.

One of the main highlights of Prison Island is the giant Aldabra tortoises, some of which are over 100 years old. These gentle creatures move slowly and peacefully, allowing visitors to get very close, take photos, and even feed them under supervision. They are one of the oldest living attractions in Zanzibar.

The island is surrounded by crystal-clear turquoise waters, making it perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and boat rides. The coral reefs around the island are home to colorful fish and marine life, offering a beautiful underwater experience for visitors.

As you walk around the island, you will also see the old colonial prison building, which adds historical value to the place. The structure tells a story of the past and gives visitors a glimpse into Zanzibar’s colonial history.

Prison Island is also a peaceful escape from the busy streets of Stone Town. The natural beauty, fresh ocean breeze, and calm environment make it a perfect place for relaxation and photography.

A visit to Prison Island is a combination of history, wildlife, and natural beauty. It is an unforgettable experience that connects visitors with Zanzibar’s heritage and the wonders of nature.
