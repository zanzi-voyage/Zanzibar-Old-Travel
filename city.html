Zanzibar City Activities

Explore the best places in Stone Town

Old Fort

The Old Fort, also known as the Arab Fort, is one of the oldest and most iconic historical buildings in Zanzibar, located in the heart of Stone Town. Built in the 17th century by the Omanis, it was originally used as a defensive structure to protect the island from foreign invasions.

Today, the Old Fort is a cultural center filled with life and activity. Inside its thick stone walls, visitors can find local shops, handmade crafts, and small galleries showcasing traditional art. It is also a popular place for cultural events, music performances, and festivals.

Walking through the Old Fort gives you a sense of Zanzibar’s rich history and heritage. The ancient walls, open courtyard, and historical atmosphere make it a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Stone Town.

House of Freddie Mercury

Freddie Mercury was one of the most iconic and talented musicians in the world, best known as the lead singer of the legendary rock band Queen. He was born in Zanzibar in 1946, making the island an important part of his early life and history.

Known for his powerful voice, unique stage presence, and unforgettable performances, Freddie Mercury became a global music legend. Songs like “Bohemian Rhapsody,” “We Will Rock You,” and “We Are the Champions” made him famous across the world.

Even today, his legacy lives on, and many visitors come to Zanzibar to see the place where he was born. His story inspires millions, showing how talent, passion, and confidence can turn dreams into reality.

Shopping

Shopping in Zanzibar is a vibrant and exciting experience that reflects the island’s rich culture and history. From the narrow streets of Stone Town to local markets and small boutiques, visitors can explore a wide variety of unique items and handmade products.

One of the most popular things to buy in Zanzibar is handmade souvenirs. These include carved wooden items, colorful paintings, traditional fabrics, jewelry, and woven baskets. Each item tells a story and represents the creativity of local artisans.

Zanzibar is also famous for its spices, making them a perfect gift to take home. You can find cloves, cinnamon, vanilla, and other spices sold in beautifully packed sets, allowing you to carry a piece of the island’s flavor with you.

Walking through the markets is an experience on its own. The friendly atmosphere, local interactions, and lively environment make shopping enjoyable and memorable. Visitors also have the chance to bargain, which is part of the local culture.

Shopping in Zanzibar is not just about buying items—it is about experiencing the culture, meeting people, and discovering the artistic spirit of the island.

Historical Church

Zanzibar is home to some of the most historic and beautiful churches in East Africa, offering visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and spirituality. One of the most famous is the Anglican Cathedral in Stone Town, built on the site of the former slave market, making it a powerful symbol of freedom and human dignity.

The architecture of these churches is truly remarkable, combining European design with local influences. High ceilings, stained glass windows, and detailed wooden carvings create a peaceful and inspiring atmosphere for visitors.

Visiting a church in Zanzibar is not only about religion—it is also about understanding the island’s history. Many churches hold deep stories connected to the past, especially during the time of the slave trade, making them important historical landmarks.

Inside, the calm and quiet environment offers a perfect place for reflection and relaxation. The soft light coming through the windows, along with the silence, creates a sense of peace that visitors truly appreciate.

A Church Tour in Zanzibar is a meaningful experience that combines history, culture, and spirituality, giving visitors a deeper connection to the island and its past.

Market Tour

Darajani Market is the busiest and most famous local market in Zanzibar, located in the heart of Stone Town. It is a vibrant place where everyday life comes alive through colors, sounds, and movement.

Here, you can find everything from fresh fruits, vegetables, spices, and seafood to local goods sold by friendly vendors. The market is always full of energy, with people buying, selling, and interacting in a lively atmosphere.

Visiting Darajani Market is a great way to experience the real culture of Zanzibar and see how locals live their daily lives.
